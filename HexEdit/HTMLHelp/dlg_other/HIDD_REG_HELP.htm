Cost

You may purchase a single-user license (license) for $40 (US dollars). You may not use a single-user license to run multiple copies of HexEdit on different machines simultaneously. For this, site (multi-user) licenses are available at a per-user discount upon request.  However, a single person may run HexEdit on different machines at different times.

What You Get

Purchasing a licence (license) entitles you to free support and upgrades for at least 2 years. It also means that if you want a specific enhancement, it will be given priority over my own ideas and those of unregistered users.  We have a history of implementing user requests.

 

And (this may sound like the standard shareware pitch :-) by buying the product you will make it better, since I can put more time into improving it. I promise that all sales are put back into development and not into a new sportscar.

 

HexEdit 1.0 was released as freeware and had an overwhelmingly positive response. Unfortunately due to work and family commitments I have been able to do very little work on it for almost 18 months. So in order to justify the time spent on the numerous requested enhancements (and my own ideas for enhancements), I have found it necessary to release new versions as shareware.

 

If you really cannot afford to pay for it we release earlier versions as freeware.

 

There really are some interesting possible enhancements in the pipeline. For example, I have had an idea, since the inception of HexEdit, for a sort of “aerial view” of a binary file – in fact the color coding of different byte values in version 1.0 was the first step in implementing this. I believe this would give a nice overview of large files and allow much easier detection of irregularities and important sections in many types of files.  By buying the product you may make these sorts of enhancements a reality.
